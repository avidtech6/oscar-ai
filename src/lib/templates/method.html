1.0 Introduction

This Arboricultural Method Statement (AMS) has been prepared to detail the working methods and protective measures required to safeguard trees during the construction phase of the development at {{project.siteAddress}}.

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this method statement is to:

  • Define the working methods to be employed near trees
  • Specify tree protection measures in accordance with BS5837:2012
  • Provide clear instructions to contractors and site personnel
  • Minimize the risk of damage to retained trees
  • Ensure compliance with planning conditions and best practice

2.0 Tree Protection Principles

2.1 Root Protection Area (RPA)

The Root Protection Area (RPA) is calculated as 12 times the stem diameter (DBH) measured at 1.5m above ground level. No construction activities, storage of materials, or soil compaction should occur within the RPA.

2.2 Tree Protection Zones (TPZ)

Tree Protection Zones shall be established around all retained trees. The TPZ shall extend to the full RPA radius plus an additional 1.5m buffer zone.

TPZ Requirements:

  • Clearly marked with fencing at 2.4m height
  • Signage indicating "Tree Protection Zone - No Entry"
  • No storage of materials within TPZ
  • No vehicle movement within TPZ
  • No excavation or ground disturbance within TPZ

3.1 Phase 1: Site Setup and Mobilization

Step 1: Site Induction

All site personnel must attend a tree protection induction before commencing work. The induction will cover:

  • Location of Tree Protection Zones
  • Prohibited activities near trees
  • Emergency procedures for tree damage
  • Reporting requirements

Step 2: Access Route Establishment

Designated access routes shall be established to avoid Tree Protection Zones. Routes shall be:

  • Clearly marked with ground protection mats
  • Located at least 3m from any TPZ boundary
  • Regularly inspected for encroachment

3.2 Phase 2: Tree Protection Installation

Step 3: Fencing Installation

Install 2.4m high Heras fencing or equivalent around all TPZs. Fencing shall be:

  • Erected before any construction activity begins
  • Maintained throughout the construction period
  • Inspected weekly for damage or unauthorized entry

Step 4: Ground Protection

Where construction activities must occur near TPZs, install ground protection:

  • Use timber mats or equivalent for temporary access
  • Ensure no soil compaction within RPA
  • Remove protection immediately after use

4.0 Monitoring and Inspection

4.1 Weekly Inspection Checklist

□
TPZ fencing intact and secure
□
No materials stored within TPZ
□
No vehicle encroachment into TPZ
□
No excavation within RPA
□
Tree condition unchanged (no damage to bark, branches, roots)
□
Ground protection mats in good condition

4.2 Emergency Procedures

In case of tree damage:

  1. Stop all work in the immediate area
  2. Notify the site supervisor and arboricultural consultant immediately
  3. Photograph the damage from multiple angles
  4. Implement temporary protection measures
  5. Do not attempt to repair the damage without specialist advice
