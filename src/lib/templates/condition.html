1.0 Introduction

This Tree Condition Report presents the findings of a Visual Tree Assessment (VTA) conducted at {{project.siteAddress}} on {{survey.date}}. The assessment follows the methodology outlined in the National Tree Safety Group's "Common Sense Risk Management of Trees".

1.1 Assessment Purpose

The purpose of this assessment is to:

  • Evaluate the structural condition and health of trees
  • Identify any defects or hazards that may pose a risk
  • Provide recommendations for tree management
  • Assess the suitability of trees for retention
  • Inform maintenance planning and risk management

1.2 Assessment Methodology

The assessment was conducted using Visual Tree Assessment (VTA) techniques, including:

Crown Assessment

Density, symmetry, dieback, epicormic growth

Stem Assessment

Bark condition, cavities, cracks, lean

Root Assessment

Root plate movement, soil conditions, excavations

Fungal Assessment

Presence of fungi, decay indicators

2.0 Risk Assessment Matrix

Low Risk

Minor defects, no immediate action required

Medium Risk

Moderate defects, monitor or consider works

High Risk

Significant defects, works recommended

Immediate Risk

Dangerous condition, urgent action required

2.1 Risk Factors Considered

  • Target: Likelihood of people/property being present
  • Probability of Failure: Likelihood of tree/part failing
  • Size of Part: Size of part likely to fail
  • Impact: Consequences of failure
