1. Introduction
1.1 Instructions

This survey has been instructed by {{project.client}} to assess trees within {{project.siteAddress}} in accordance with BS5837:2012 "Trees in relation to design, demolition and construction - Recommendations".

1.2 Scope of Survey

The survey includes all trees within the site boundary and those off-site whose Root Protection Areas (RPAs) may be affected by proposed development.

1.3 Limitations
  • This is a preliminary tree survey (Phase 1)
  • No invasive soil excavations or climbing inspections were carried out
  • Trees have been assessed from ground level only
2. Survey Methodology
2.1 Tree Assessment

Each tree was assessed for:

  • Species: Common name and scientific name
  • Height: Estimated in metres
  • Stem Diameter: Measured at 1.5m above ground level (or as specified)
  • Crown Spread: Measured at cardinal points (N, E, S, W)
  • Crown Clearance: Height of crown above ground level
  • Age Class: Young / Semi-mature / Mature / Over-mature / Veteran
  • Condition: Physical condition (good, fair, poor, dead)
  • Structural Condition: Assessment of defects and management requirements
2.2 Category Grading

Trees are graded in accordance with BS5837:2012:

Category Description
A High quality and value - retention desirable
B Moderate quality and value - retention desirable
C Low quality and value - retention acceptable
U Unsuitable for retention

4. Root Protection Area (RPA)
4.1 Calculation

RPA calculated in accordance with BS5837:2012 as:

  • Circular method: 12 × stem diameter at 1.5m
  • Area calculation: π × radius²
